Sushi

Description

This is a classic California roll recipe, great for those who are just learning to make sushi. Serve as an appetizer or as part of a Japanese meal

Ingredients

  • ½ cucumber
  • 3 ½ ounces crab sticks, finely chopped
  • 4 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 4 sheets nori (dry seaweed)
  • 2 ½ cups cooked sushi rice
  • 2 ½ tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 4 teaspoons tobiko (flying fish roe)

Steps

  • Step 1
    1. Slice the ends off of the cucumber, then cut into quarters lengthwise
    2. Thinly slice each quarter lengthwise to make spears
  • Step 2
    1. Stir chopped crab sticks and mayonnaise together in a small bowl
  • Step 3
    1. Lay 1 sheet of nori on a bamboo mat
    2. Pat a thin, even layer of rice over the nori with wet hands, leaving a 1/3-inch edge at the bottom
    3. Sprinkle rice with sesame seeds. Carefully flip over
  • Step 4
    1. Add 1 tablespoon of the crab mixture in a line across the sheet about 1/3 of the way from the top
    2. Layer with 1 to 2 cucumber spears and 1 to 3 slices of avocado
  • Step 5
    1. Using the bamboo mat, roll the sheet tightly into a cylinder, starting at the top edge
    2. Moisten the uncovered edge with water and finish rolling once you reached the bottom
    3. Repeat with the remaining ingredients
  • Step 6
    1. Slice each roll into 3/4-inch pieces
    2. Garnish each piece with 1/8 teaspoon tobiko
